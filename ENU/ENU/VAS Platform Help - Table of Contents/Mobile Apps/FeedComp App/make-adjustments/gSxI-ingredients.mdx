<ExpandableHeading>
  ## Create New Ingredient

  <hint type="info">
    **TIP:&#x20;**&#x45;ach ingredient must have at least one \{\{FeedComp.FeedCompFeatures-Lower}}. Be sure to add your [Locations](<../../../../../VAS Platform Help - Table of Contents/Mobile Apps/FeedComp App/Make Adjustments/locations.mdx>) before adding ingredients. See [Before You Begin: Required Setup](<../../../../../VAS Platform Help - Table of Contents/Mobile Apps/FeedComp App/before-you-begin-required-setup.mdx>) to learn more about the required setup order.
  </hint>

  1. Tap `Create Ingredient` at the bottom of the screen.
  2. Enter the **Ingredient Name**.
  3. If you're adding a \{\{FeedComp.FeedCompWeighback-lower}} ingredient, tap the \{\{FeedComp.FeedCompWeighback-singular}} \{\{FeedComp.FeedCompIngredient-singular}} toggle.
  4. Select the **Ingredient Type**.
  5. Enter the **Load Tolerance**.
  6. Tap **+ Assign Location** in the middle of the screen.
  7. Select the location, select the **Unit** or **Ton** option, enter the **DM%**, and **Cost Unit** or **Cost Ton**, \{\{Commands.UserActions}}`Add` at the bottom of the screen.
  8. \{\{Commands.UserActions}}`Save` at the bottom of the screen.
</ExpandableHeading>

  1. \{\{Commands.Click}} the \{\{FeedComp.FeedCompFeatures-Lower}} to \{\{Commands.UserActions}}.

  3. Update the **Ingredient Type**, \{\{Commands.UserActions}}`Save` at the bottom of the screen.

  <ExpandableHeading>
    ### Click here to learn more about ingredient types!

    - Dry Forage: Vegetative material, such as stems, leaves, and stalks of plants, in a dried state that is used for feed.
    - Fresh Forage: A crop of cultivated plants or plant parts that are grazed or harvested as feed.
    - Silage: Preserved forage for feed, like pasture grass, alfalfa, grains, and corn, that is produced by fermenting plant matter.
    - Energy: High-calorie feed, such as corn, sorghum, barley, and wheat.
    - Protein: High-protein feed, such as soy and canola.
    - Vitamins and Minerals: Supplements added to feed to prevent deficiencies in \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.d-airy}} cows that can impact health and milk production.
    - Additives: Products added to feed to enhance feed quality, make the feed more digestible, and improve health and performance.
    - Water: Water added to a feed mixture to improve daily milk yield.
  </ExpandableHeading>
