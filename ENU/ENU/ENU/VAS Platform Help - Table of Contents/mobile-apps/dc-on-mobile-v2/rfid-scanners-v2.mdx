<ExpandableHeading>
  ## Use a Scanner with Android Devices

  Make sure that Bluetooth is enabled both in your mobile device and the scanner, and then tap the **More** button in the mobile app to access the following scanner options.

  If the scanner you want to use is already paired with your device but shows a status of **Not Connected**, make sure that the scanner is powered on and that the Bluetooth connection is enabled in both your mobile device and the scanner. For any scanner that shows a status of **Connected**, you can use it to look up animals in the mobile app to [Complete Tasks (v2)](<../../../../../VAS Platform Help - Table of Contents/Mobile Apps/DC on Mobile (v2)/complete-tasks-v2.mdx>) and [Enter Events (v2)](<../../../../../VAS Platform Help - Table of Contents/Mobile Apps/DC on Mobile (v2)/enter-events-v2.mdx>).

  <ExpandableHeading>
    ### Show me how to pair a new scanner

    When pairing with available scanners, simply tap any scanner in the list that is **Not Connected**. If the scanner has not paired with your device yet, you may be prompted to enter a PIN to complete the pairing.

    If your \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.d-airy}} has multiple scanners and wands, you can pair each of them with your device and then select the one you want to use within the \{\{General.ProductName}} mobile app.
  </ExpandableHeading>

  <ExpandableHeading>
    ### Look for the Scanner Shortcut!

    In the \{\{MorphHelp.AndroidOS-TM}} version of the mobile app, features that support scanners, such as [completing tasks](<../../../../../VAS Platform Help - Table of Contents/Mobile Apps/DC on Mobile (v2)/complete-tasks-v2.mdx>) and [entering events](<../../../../../VAS Platform Help - Table of Contents/Mobile Apps/DC on Mobile (v2)/enter-events-v2.mdx>), offer a shortcut for using scanners.

    Toggle the **Scanner** connection switch on or off to connect or disconnect from a paired scanner. When you toggle this switch on, the mobile app detects which scanner was last used under your account, and it connects you to it. Once the scanner finishes connecting, the mobile app displays a pop-up message to confirm that it is ready to use.

    Note that in the \{\{MorphHelp.AppleOS-TM}} version of the mobile app, the **Scanner** indicator is not a switch that you can toggle. Instead, it is a status indicator that shows green when a scanner is connected and gray when it is not.
  </ExpandableHeading>

  <ExpandableHeading>
    ## I don't see the More button. How do I find it?

    Follow the steps below if you do not see the \{\{Mobile-UI.Feature-Names}} button at the bottom of your screen.

    1. Tap the **Back** arrow at the top left of the screen until you return to one of the main feature screens.
    2. From there, you'll find the feature buttons at the bottom of the screen.

  ## Troubleshoot Scanner Connections

  If the mobile app loses connection with your scanner, check its connection status on the \{\{Mobile-UI.MoreTab}} menu under \*\*\{\{masterVAS-Terminology.radiofrequencyID-short}}\*\***Scanner**. Expand the topics below for help understanding the connection status:

  <ExpandableHeading>
    ### \<Scanner Name> Ready

    The wand name is displayed and **Ready**, and the connection switch is active. This means that the Bluetooth connection is active for both the mobile device and \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.radiofrequencyID-short}} wand, and both devices are powered on. The wand is connected and ready to scan data to the mobile app.
  </ExpandableHeading>

  <ExpandableHeading>
    ### Connected to \<Scanner Name>

    The \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.radiofrequencyID-short}} wand is paired to the mobile device but is not currently connected to the mobile app.

    On Android devices, tap the \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.radiofrequencyID-short}} menu item to connect the wand, and for \{\{MorphHelp.AppleOS}} devices, simply make sure the wand is powered on and has Bluetooth enabled. Once the wand connects to the mobile app, the connection switch is active.
  </ExpandableHeading>

  <ExpandableHeading>
    ### Starting...

    On \{\{MorphHelp.AndroidOS}} devices, this message appears when you tap the \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.radiofrequencyID-short}} menu item to connect the scanner. \{\{MorphHelp.AppleOS}} devices will automatically initiate the scanner connection. In either case, the scanner typically connects quickly, so this message may be displayed only very briefly.
  </ExpandableHeading>

  <ExpandableHeading>
    ### Not Connected

    The fully grayed-out \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.radiofrequencyID-short}} menu item most likely indicates one the following issues:

    - No wands are currently paired with the mobile device.
    - The wand's Bluetooth connection is turned off.
    - The mobile device's Bluetooth connection is turned off.
  </ExpandableHeading>

  <hint type="info">
    **TIP:&#x20;**&#x49;f you turn on **Airplane Mode** on your \{\{MorphHelp.AppleOS-TM}} device, Bluetooth is turned off automatically. You can turn Bluetooth back on in settings, and it will stay on until you turn it off or toggle Airplane Mode off and on again.
  </hint>
</ExpandableHeading>

  ## Tips for Using Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE)

  \{\{masterVAS-Products.DConMobile}} supports scanners that use \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.BLElong}}. If the device connecting to the app supports \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.BLEshort}}, the device will automatically use it. Otherwise, the device will deactivate \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.BLEshort}} and use Bluetooth Classic instead.

  You may see a Bluetooth Pairing Request message or encounter some issues when you try to connect a \{\{masterVAS-Products.DConMobile}}-compatible scanner to \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.BLEshort}}. Follow the guidelines below when troubleshooting or connecting to \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.BLEshort}}:

  - \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.SRS2long}} and \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.XRS2long}}\:If your scanner was connected to Bluetooth Classic and you try to switch the scanner connection to \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.BLEshort}}, the app may not detect the scanner over \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.BLEshort}}. To fix this, turn the scanner off and on. If that doesn't work, remove the scanner from your saved scanners list under System Settings and try again.
  - \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.SRS2ilong}} and \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.XRS2ilong}}\:The \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.SRS2ishort}} and \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.XRS2ishort}} have both \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.BLEshort}} and Bluetooth Classic modules. \{\{MorphHelp.AppleOS-TM}}**&#x20;devices only support&#x20;**\{\{masterVAS-Terminology.BLEshort}}**&#x20;connections with these scanners**. When you first connect, the scanner enables both protocols but then disables the unused one. This can cause the \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.BLEshort}} module not to start, and the app won't detect the scanner over \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.BLEshort}}. To fix this, turn the scanner off and on. If that doesn't work, remove the scanner from your saved scanners list under System Settings and try again.
