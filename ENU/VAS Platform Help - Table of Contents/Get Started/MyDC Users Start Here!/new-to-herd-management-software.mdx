We appreciate that you chose \{\{masterVAS-Products.Pulse-only}} to manage your \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.h-erd}}! Expand the topics below to become familiar with the concepts of \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.h-erd}} management:

<ExpandableHeading>
  ## CowCards & items

  A \{\{masterVAS-Products.Pulse-only}} CowCard contains detailed status and event information about each animal in your \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.h-erd}}, including reproduction status and days in milk, as well as any milk and meat withholds. Use CowCards to quickly access animal details.

  <Image src="https://archbee-image-uploads-qa.s3.amazonaws.com/QjalyvC_Eyr95bGir3p2_/V4DYSaiOZq-8bn56QLzWJ_cowcard-overview.png" size="93" isUploading="false" initialPath="../../Resources/Images/Platform/cowcards/cowcard-overview.png" githubPath="Content/Resources/Images/Platform/cowcards/cowcard-overview.png" position="flex-start" showCaption="false"></Image>

  CowCards use the following common terms, which are known as "items" in \{\{masterVAS-Products.Pulse-only}}: 

  - \{\{LACT}}: Lactation number
  - \{\{RPRO}}: Reproductive code (or status)
  - \{\{DIM}}: Days in milk in current lactation
  - \{\{DSLH}}: Days since last heat
  - \{\{DCC}}: Days carrying calf/pregnant
  - \{\{DUE}}: Date due to \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.f-reshen}} (if pregnant)

  See [View Animal Details on CowCards](../Features/CowCards/WorkingWithCowCards.htm) to learn more!
</ExpandableHeading>

<ExpandableHeading>
  ## Adding animal events

  \{\{masterVAS-Products.Pulse-only}} events capture key animal activities, including reproduction-related items (\{\{masterVAS-Terminology.f-reshening}}, pregnancy, breeding), location information (movement between \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.p-ens}}), and illness details (diagnoses, treatment) that are vital to managing your \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.h-erd}}. You can add animal events from web or mobile.
</ExpandableHeading>

<ExpandableHeading>
  ## What are reports and worklists?

  You can use \{\{masterVAS-Products.Pulse-only}} to generate reports, which are important analysis tools that allow you to identify animals that meet specific conditions. For instance, you may be interested in capturing a list of cows that are due to \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.f-reshen}} soon. To do that, select \{\{Buttons-UI.FeaturesMain}}**&#xA0;>&#x20;**\{\{Buttons-UI.DC-Reports}}**&#x20;> Template&#x20;**\{\{Buttons-UI.GalleryTab}} and customize the *Animals Due to&#x20;*\{\{masterVAS-Terminology.F-reshen-}} report. Note that reports are shaded in **blue** and \{\{Worklists.worklistCommonUse}}s are shaded in **green**.

  <Image src="https://archbee-image-uploads-qa.s3.amazonaws.com/QjalyvC_Eyr95bGir3p2_/g4LfxOZDhl_DfAyKr-2I5_run-report-see-results.png" size="77" isUploading="false" initialPath="../../Resources/Images/Platform/dairycomp/Reports/run-report-see-results.png" githubPath="Content/Resources/Images/Platform/dairycomp/Reports/run-report-see-results.png" position="flex-start" showCaption="false"></Image>

  You can also use \{\{masterVAS-Products.Pulse-only}} to generate \{\{Worklists.worklistCommonUse}}s, which help you track hands-on tasks that need to be done at your \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.d-airy}}.

  \{\{Buttons-UI.DC-WorklistsONLY}} capture specific actions that need to be applied to your animals, and allow you to mark animals as **DONE** when you've completed your task. For instance, you may want to go check on animals that recently \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.f-reshened}}. To do that, select \{\{Buttons-UI.FeaturesMain}}**&#xA0;>&#x20;**\{\{Buttons-UI.DC-WorklistsONLY}}**&#x20;>&#x20;**\{\{masterVAS-Terminology.D-airy-}} \{\{Buttons-UI.DC-Reports}}, run the *Recently&#x20;*\{\{masterVAS-Terminology.F-resh-cow-}}*&#x20;Cows*\{\{Worklists.worklistCommonUse}} report, and click the **Create Open Worklist** button to create an open worklist.

  Then, process animals in the list using the mobile app while out on the farm and use \{\{masterVAS-Products.Pulse-only}} to track \{\{Worklists.worklistCommonUse}} progress.

<ExpandableHeading>
  ## What's the difference between reports and worklists?

  The main difference is that worklists are actionable, while reports are for informational purposes only. Use \{\{Worklists.worklistCommonUse}}s to complete tasks, such as animal checks or treatments, and reports to analyze different aspects of your \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.h-erd}} and to drive management decisions.
</ExpandableHeading>
