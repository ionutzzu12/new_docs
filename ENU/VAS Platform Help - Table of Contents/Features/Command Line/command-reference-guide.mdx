Commands must follow a \{\{masterVAS-Products.Pulse-only}}-specific format, which may vary from \{\{DC-UI.DC-ProductName}}'s command language and structure. Expand the sections below to learn more:

<ExpandableHeading>
  ## Batch command

  To run multiple commands simultaneously, use an exclamation point (!) to separate commands.
</ExpandableHeading>

<ExpandableHeading>
  ## Case-insensitive

  Command text is not case-sensitive.
</ExpandableHeading>

<ExpandableHeading>
  ## Date format

  \{\{masterVAS-Products.Pulse-only}} interprets the order of month and day based on your [User Profile](../Config/EditUserAcct.htm)**Date format** setting.

  <Image src="https://archbee-image-uploads-qa.s3.amazonaws.com/QjalyvC_Eyr95bGir3p2_/ez6lT8cXqmcKohjP9m9ET_user-profile-date-format.png" size="20" isUploading="false" initialPath="../../../Resources/Images/Platform/users-roles/user-profile-date-format.png" githubPath="Content/Resources/Images/Platform/users-roles/user-profile-date-format.png" position="flex-start" showCaption="false"></Image>

  The year may be two or four digits, and month and day may be one or two digits. Use a forward slash (/) or period (.) to separate year, month, and day. Dates may be specified in any of the following formats: 

  | - yyyy/mm/dd or yyyy.mm.dd |
  | -------------------------- |

  - mm/dd/yy or mm.dd.yy
  - mm/dd/yyyy or mm.dd.yyyy
  - dd/mm/yy or dd.mm.yy
  - mm/dd or dd/mm (assumes the current year) | \* yyyy/m/d or yyyy.m.d
  - m/d/yy or m.d.yy
  - m/d/yyyy or m.d.yyyy
  - d/m/yy or d.m.yy
  - m/d or d/m (assumes the current year) |

  For instance, \{\{masterVAS.month09}} 17, 2019, can be formatted as:

  - 2019/09/17, 9.17.19, 9/17/19, or 9/17 if your date format is set to Month/Day/Year
  - 2019/17/09, 17.9.19, 17/9/19, or 17/9 if your date format is set to Day/Month/Year
</ExpandableHeading>

<ExpandableHeading>
  ## Date shortcuts

  \{\{DC-UI.DC-ProdNameShort}} and \{\{masterVAS-Products.Pulse-only}} accommodate date shortcuts to speed up the typing of commands. Date shortcuts may be used for any date items and work as follows: 

  - The number is the date in the current month (if \{\{today}} or already occurred)
  - The number is a date in the previous month (if not yet occurred)

  For instance, if you enter MKDAT=3, and today is \{\{masterVAS.month10}} 3, then the results show milk withdrawals ending today. If you enter FDAT>14 and today is \{\{masterVAS.month10}} 1, the results include animals with \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.f-resh-cow}} dates of \{\{masterVAS.month09}} 15 through \{\{masterVAS.month10}} 1.
</ExpandableHeading>

<ExpandableHeading>
  ## Double quotes

  Double quotes (") are required to identify a string in the command line. For instance, CODA="TEST".
</ExpandableHeading>

<ExpandableHeading>
  ## ID

  The animal \{\{ID}} is always included in LIST (SHOW) results, even if not specified in the command.
</ExpandableHeading>

<ExpandableHeading>
  ## Sort and condition keyword order

  Sort and condition keywords (BY, DOWNBY, FOR) must follow a prescribed order in LIST (SHOW) and SUM commands.

  <hint type="warning">
    **IMPORTANT:&#x20;**&#x4C;IST (SHOW) commands require that the FOR keyword is typed before the BY or DOWNBY keyword. SUM commands require that the FOR keyword is typed after the BY or DOWNBY keyword.
  </hint>
</ExpandableHeading>

<ExpandableHeading>
  ## Multiple conditional items

  AND is implied between multiple conditional items. For instance, `FORLACT>0 DCC>220 DDAT=2019/07/30` includes all results where the following is true: lactation number is greater than zero AND days carrying calf is less than 220 days AND \{\{due}} date is \{\{masterVAS.month07}} 30, 2019.
</ExpandableHeading>

<ExpandableHeading>
  ## OR conditions

  Use a semicolon (;) to indicate OR. For instance, PEN=1;3;5 includes \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.p-ens}} 1 OR 3 OR 5.

  To use compound OR conditions, no semicolon (;) is required. Separate each condition with parenthesis (). For instance, `FOR(LACT>0 DDAT=2019/07/30) (COD1>0 COD2=0)` includes results where the following is true: lactation number is greater than zero and due date is \{\{masterVAS.month07}} 30, 2019 OR \{\{COD1}} is greater than zero and \{\{COD2}} is equal to zero.
</ExpandableHeading>

<ExpandableHeading>
  ## Spacing

  Extra spaces between items are ignored.
</ExpandableHeading>

<ExpandableHeading>
  ## Switches

  In \{\{masterVAS-Products.Pulse-only}} command line, any switches must immediately follow the command word. The following switches are currently supported in the specified command(s): 

  | Switch | Definition                                                                                                                                                                                     | Command(s)          | Example                                                                                                                                                        |
  | ------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
  | \B     | Includes results for both live and dead animals. Without the switch, command results include live animals only.                                                                                | COUNTLIST (SHOW)SUM | LIST \B ID LACT DIM RPRO ARDAT FOR FDAT>-7 BY  LACTCapture live and dead animal data for recently \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.f-reshened}} animals by lactation. |
  | \D     | Limits results to dead cows only.                                                                                                                                                              | COUNTLIST (SHOW)SUM | COUNT \D FOR LACT=1 ARDAT>-90Capture total number of dead animals in their first lactation in the last 90 days.                                                |
  | \S     | Sets the \{\{standard deviation}} calculation to use sample values. Without the switch, \{\{masterVAS-Products.Pulse-only}} calculates population standard deviation.                          | SUM                 | SUM \S MILK PCTF PCTS LGSCC BY lact FOR LACT>0Analyze sample standard deviation data for milk composition and quality by lactation.                            |
  | \Z     | Includes zero values in averages and standard deviation calculations. Without the switch, average, standard deviation, \{\{mode}}, and \{\{median}} calculations include non-zero values only. | SUM                 | SUM \Z TKET BY LCTGP FOR FDAT>-365Analyze the number of times \{\{ketosis}} was diagnosed by lactation group for all animals that calved in the last year.     |
</ExpandableHeading>
