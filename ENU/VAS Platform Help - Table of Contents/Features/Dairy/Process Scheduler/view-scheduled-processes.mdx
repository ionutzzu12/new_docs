On the \{\{Protocols.ProcessScheduler}} page, you can see all your scheduled processes in one easy-to-read calendar. In a few clicks, you can also see when each process is scheduled to start or how often the process is scheduled to run.

![](https://archbee-image-uploads-qa.s3.amazonaws.com/QjalyvC_Eyr95bGir3p2_/e5EIuNEBlfEMPZu2hhHdv_viewprocesssched.png)

Follow the guidelines below when working with the \{\{Protocols.ProcessScheduler}} calendar:

- Use the filters on the upper left side of the calendar to select the processes you want to see on the schedule.
- Click the \{\{TODAY}} button on the upper left side of the calendar to see the current month's scheduled processes.
- Click the **Add Scheduled Process** button on the upper right side of the calendar to schedule a new process.:::hint\{type="info"}
  **NOTE:&#x20;**&#x43;urrently, users can only add a \{\{Protocols.Protocol-singular}} task status alert from the \{\{Protocols.ProcessScheduler}} page. See [Add and Manage Task Status Alerts](AddManageAlerts.htm) to learn more about scheduling the alert messages.
  \:::

Additionally, you can manage your system's processes directly on the \{\{Protocols.ProcessScheduler}} page. To learn more about scheduling and editing the processes shown on your \{\{Protocols.ProcessScheduler}} calendar, see [Schedule or Edit a Process](ScheduleEditProcess.htm).
