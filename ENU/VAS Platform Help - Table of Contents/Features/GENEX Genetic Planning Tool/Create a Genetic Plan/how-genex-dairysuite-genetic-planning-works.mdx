  ## How are trait averages evaluated?

  Most trait averages are evaluated using three-generation parent averages, but some trait averages are evaluated using one-generation parent average or a breed average. The use of parent or breed averages depends on the type of animal that is being evaluated:

  - **Three-generation parent average:** If a proof is not available for a female, trait data is used from three previous \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.s-ire}} generations (\{\{masterVAS-Terminology.s-ire}}, maternal \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.g-rands-ire}}, and maternal great-\{\{masterVAS-Terminology.g-rands-ire}}) to calculate the female's trait averages. \{\{UDS-general.UDSBrand}}\{\{UDS-general.UDSProduct}} calculates the trait values using weighted percentages of the three-generation parent averages and the breed average:\* 50% \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.s-ire}}
    - 25% maternal \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.g-rands-ire}}
    - 12.5% maternal great-\{\{masterVAS-Terminology.g-rands-ire}}
    - 12.5% breed average
  - **One-generation parent average:** To calculate a potential progeny's trait averages, trait data is used from the progeny's parents (\{\{masterVAS-Terminology.s-ire}} and \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.d-am}}).
  - **Breed average:** When parent data is unavailable, a progeny's trait averages are based on the breed average.
</ExpandableHeading>

<ExpandableHeading>
  ## How are genetic values projected for future progeny?

  During genetic planning, we collect the genetic values for all females and calculate the average of the collected female data. During [Step 4: Create Your Strategy](step-4-create-your-strategy.mdx), we collect the genetic values for all bulls and calculate the average of the collected bull data. We then use the average female value and the average bull value to project the progeny's genetic values. After mating, we use the bull’s actual genetic values to predict the progeny’s genetic values.
</ExpandableHeading>

<ExpandableHeading>
  ## How is inbreeding evaluated and prevented?

  - **Evaluation:** To ensure that inbreeding information is accurate, inbreeding values are evaluated using seven generations of the progeny's pedigree.
  - **Prevention:** The mating plan always performs a check to ensure the animal's \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.s-ire}} is not her assigned mating \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.s-ire}}. Additionally, you can set additional restrictions and protections to prevent inbreeding in :Link[Step 4: Create Your Strategy]{label="Step 4: Create Your Strategy" overridedLabel="Step 4: Create Your Strategy" spaceId docId="Q6qb0xcUw4LyE67OtGMPX" version="v2" docAnchorId="#restricts" loadingMethod="dynamic" newTab="false" githubPath="GeneticPlanStep4.htm#restricts" href="step-4-create-your-strategy.mdx"}, such as customizing the maximum allowed inbreeding percentage.
</ExpandableHeading>

<ExpandableHeading>
  ## How is trait data used to match bulls to females in the mating list?

  The plan's individual females' traits are analyzed and \{\{UDS-general.UDSBrand}}\{\{UDS-general.UDSProduct}} identifies their three traits that are furthest from the plan goals. Then, bulls are matched to each female to improve the identified traits in their potential progeny. Before selection, \{\{UDS-general.UDSBrand}}\{\{UDS-general.UDSProduct}} prohibits any bull and female match that would result in recessive traits, inbreeding, or negative haplotypes (see the full list below). In these calculations, trait data is based on one-generation parent (\{\{masterVAS-Terminology.s-ire}} and \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.d-am}}) trait averages.
