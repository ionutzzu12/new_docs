<ExpandableHeading>
  ### Required Permissions

  Only users who are assigned to a Role with \{\{RolesPermissions.PermissionNames}} permissions can use this feature. See [Edit Dairy User Permissions](../Config/UserMgmtRoles/EditUserPermissions.htm) and [Manage Dairy Roles](<../../../../Manage Dairy Users and Roles/manage-dairy-roles.mdx>) for more information.
</ExpandableHeading>

These instructions continue where [Step 2: Enter Alert Thresholds](step-2-enter-alert-thresholds.mdx) ends and show you how to select the events you want \{\{masterVAS-Products.Pulse-only}} to track and the team members you want to notify when your \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.h-erd}} exceeds the alert thresholds.

\{\{masterVAS-Products.Pulse-only}} does not currently support alerts and notifications for all available indicator types.

You cannot add or edit alert groups until \{\{PlatformContentSnippets.PerformanceIndicators-AlertsAndThresholds}} are enabled in \{\{masterVAS-Products.Pulse-only}} and at least one \{\{PlatformContentSnippets.PerformanceIndicators-AlertThresholdValue}} is entered on the [Indicators](../working-with-indicators.mdx) page for your \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.h-erd}}.

See [Step 1: Enable & Set Up Health Indicators](Step1Indicators.htm) and [Step 2: Enter Alert Thresholds](step-2-enter-alert-thresholds.mdx) for instructions.

Follow the steps below to continue:

1. Select the \{\{Buttons-UI.AlertGroups}} tab on the [Performance Indicators](<../../../../../../ENU/VAS Platform Help - Table of Contents/Features/Dairy/settings-dairy/performance-indicators.mdx>) page.
2. To add a new alert group for your \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.d-airy}}, click the **Add Alert Group** button. \{\{masterVAS-Products.Pulse-only}} opens a side panel where you can configure the alert group.:inlineImage[]{src="https://archbee-image-uploads-qa.s3.amazonaws.com/QjalyvC_Eyr95bGir3p2_/2WCpTw6umSuu5YxQ8_Kmy_indicators-tab-alerts-subtab-blank-addintro.png" alt caption size="63"}
3. Enter an **Alert Group Name** for the alert group. For example, since we entered an \{\{PlatformContentSnippets.PerformanceIndicators-AlertThresholdValue}} for the Overall \{\{Mastitis}} and Cow Mastitis events in [Step 2: Enter Alert Thresholds](step-2-enter-alert-thresholds.mdx), we'll enter an alert group name of "Mastitis Events Alert Group" here.:inlineImage[]{src="https://archbee-image-uploads-qa.s3.amazonaws.com/QjalyvC_Eyr95bGir3p2_/Ydfs87Dthm4eJ2GXJdysV_indicators-tab-alerts-subtab-add-name.png" alt caption size="37"}
4. While still in the side panel, use the **Indicators to Track** area to include one or more \{\{PlatformContentSnippets.PerformanceIndicators-EventIndicator}}s to track across your \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.h-erd}} for this alert group.Continuing with our example from [Step 2: Enter Alert Thresholds](step-2-enter-alert-thresholds.mdx), we'll add **Overall Mastitis** and **Cow Mastitis** as the \{\{PlatformContentSnippets.PerformanceIndicators-EventIndicator}}s we want this alert group to track.:inlineImage[]{src="https://archbee-image-uploads-qa.s3.amazonaws.com/QjalyvC_Eyr95bGir3p2_/zKwGmgNxU_0OV9oGf1sGS_indicators-tab-alerts-subtab-add-omcm.png" alt caption size="42"}Check out some pointers on adding \{\{PlatformContentSnippets.PerformanceIndicators-EventIndicator}}s to this alert group\:Check the box to select the **Overall Mastitis**\{\{PlatformContentSnippets.PerformanceIndicators-EventIndicator}}. This adds the event to the **Indicators to Track** area and updates the total count of events being tracked.:inlineImage[]{src="https://archbee-image-uploads-qa.s3.amazonaws.com/QjalyvC_Eyr95bGir3p2_/SvULEte7nn4GYViQJ6WG4_indicators-tab-alerts-subtab-add-om.png" alt caption size="39"}:inlineImage[]{src="https://archbee-image-uploads-qa.s3.amazonaws.com/QjalyvC_Eyr95bGir3p2_/KIrq_1W9aMYMtYdnAwAJM_indicators-tab-alerts-subtab-add-om-slct.png" alt caption size="41"}Check the box to select the **Cow Mastitis**\{\{PlatformContentSnippets.PerformanceIndicators-EventIndicator}}. This adds the event to the **Indicators to Track** area and updates the total count of events being tracked.:inlineImage[]{src="https://archbee-image-uploads-qa.s3.amazonaws.com/QjalyvC_Eyr95bGir3p2_/iXkifofQhIku5iHYkNbnb_indicators-tab-alerts-subtab-add-cm.png" alt caption size="39"}:inlineImage[]{src="https://archbee-image-uploads-qa.s3.amazonaws.com/QjalyvC_Eyr95bGir3p2_/5GUxAYwhTMeyL-ijJEcjY_indicators-tab-alerts-subtab-add-cm-slct.png" alt caption size="40"}If you add an \{\{PlatformContentSnippets.PerformanceIndicators-EventIndicator}} to an alert group, and the indicator does not have an \{\{PlatformContentSnippets.PerformanceIndicators-AlertThresholdValue}}, \{\{masterVAS-Products.Pulse-only}} will still allow you to add it to the alert group, but it will notify you that there is no \{\{PlatformContentSnippets.PerformanceIndicators-AlertThresholdValue}} for it.For example, since we didn't add an \{\{PlatformContentSnippets.PerformanceIndicators-AlertThresholdValue}} for the \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.f-resh-cow}} cow mastitis event in [Step 2: Enter Alert Thresholds](step-2-enter-alert-thresholds.mdx), check the box to select the \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.F-resh-cow-}}**&#x20;Cow Mastitis**\{\{PlatformContentSnippets.PerformanceIndicators-EventIndicator}}. This adds the event to the **Indicators to Track** area and updates the total count of events being tracked. In addition, an icon displays next to the event, which lets you know that an \{\{PlatformContentSnippets.PerformanceIndicators-AlertThresholdValue}} has not been entered on the [Indicators](../working-with-indicators.mdx) page for the event.:inlineImage[]{src="https://archbee-image-uploads-qa.s3.amazonaws.com/QjalyvC_Eyr95bGir3p2_/8j2PGbTn3KT746KM7iO_a_indicators-tab-alerts-subtab-add-fcm-slct.png" alt caption size="46"}This means that \{\{masterVAS-Products.Pulse-only}} won't track or send alerts for this \{\{PlatformContentSnippets.PerformanceIndicators-EventIndicator}} until you enter an \{\{PlatformContentSnippets.PerformanceIndicators-AlertThresholdValue}} for the event on the [Indicators](../working-with-indicators.mdx) page.You can leave the \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.F-resh-cow-}}**&#x20;Cow Mastitis** event in the alert group, save the alert group, return to the [Indicators](../working-with-indicators.mdx) page, and enter an \{\{PlatformContentSnippets.PerformanceIndicators-AlertThresholdValue}} for it. This makes the icon go away and \{\{masterVAS-Products.Pulse-only}} will begin tracking and sending alerts for it.For this example, let's click the **X** on the \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.F-resh-cow-}}**&#x20;Cow Mastitis** event to delete it from the alert group. The event is removed from the **Indicators to Track** area and the total count of events being tracked is reduced.:inlineImage[]{src="https://archbee-image-uploads-qa.s3.amazonaws.com/QjalyvC_Eyr95bGir3p2_/up9uhGv3AF3h9NI_iF9lb_indicators-tab-alerts-subtab-add-fcm-delete.png" alt caption size="43"}:inlineImage[]{src="https://archbee-image-uploads-qa.s3.amazonaws.com/QjalyvC_Eyr95bGir3p2_/UaycO37HKIv0vGsyzBaTk_indicators-tab-alerts-subtab-add-fcm-deleted.png" alt caption size="42"}
5. Now let's select which members of your \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.d-airy}} team \{\{masterVAS-Products.Pulse-only}} will alert for these mastitis events. You can do this in the **Users to Notify** area of the side panel.:inlineImage[]{src="https://archbee-image-uploads-qa.s3.amazonaws.com/QjalyvC_Eyr95bGir3p2_/HmFtmJgdqHym7uQvdlfYz_indicators-tab-alerts-subtab-add-users.png" alt caption size="41"}Multiple members of your \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.d-airy}} team can be added to the to the **Users to Notify** area for an alert group. Generic user names were added in this example, but you can select your name and/or the name of any member of your \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.d-airy}} team.:inlineImage[]{src="https://archbee-image-uploads-qa.s3.amazonaws.com/QjalyvC_Eyr95bGir3p2_/lBgu_3FbTq01Aw-GrJ2zj_indicators-tab-alerts-subtab-add-users-slct2.png" alt caption size="40"}If a member of your \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.d-airy}} team has both a mobile phone number and an email address recorded in their user account, both the **SMS** and **Email** boxes are automatically checked as the default when the user is selected.:::hint\{type="info"}
   **NOTE:&#x20;**&#x54;he **SMS** box cannot be checked for a user who does not have a mobile phone number listed in account settings. Additionally, \{\{masterVAS-Products.Pulse-only}} cannot send SMS alerts to international phone numbers at this time, but they can still receive email alerts!
   \:::If a member of your \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.d-airy}} team prefers to be alerted only by text message, you can uncheck the **Email** box.:inlineImage[]{src="https://archbee-image-uploads-qa.s3.amazonaws.com/QjalyvC_Eyr95bGir3p2_/zQzPoMrpNVG7C3IAjZlHt_indicators-tab-alerts-subtab-add-email-dslct.png" alt caption size="38"}:::hint\{type="info"}
   **NOTE:&#x20;**\{\{masterVAS-Products.Pulse-only}} sends notifications for alert groups only by text or email. Alert group notifications are not added to the [Message Center](../MessageCenter/MessageCenter.htm) or to the [Notifications button](<../../../../../FAQs/Using PULSE/how-do-notifications-work.mdx>) as a new activity badge.
   \:::Now that you've told \{\{masterVAS-Products.Pulse-only}} who you want to alert and how to notify them, the last thing you need to do is tell \{\{masterVAS-Products.Pulse-only}} what time of day to examine \{\{PlatformContentSnippets.PerformanceIndicators-ThresholdDifferential}}s and send alerts to your \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.d-airy}} team.
6. While still in the side panel, use the **Time Preferences** area to select the time of day when \{\{masterVAS-Products.Pulse-only}} examines \{\{PlatformContentSnippets.PerformanceIndicators-ThresholdDifferential}}s for your \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.h-erd}} and notifies your \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.d-airy}} team.:inlineImage[]{src="https://archbee-image-uploads-qa.s3.amazonaws.com/QjalyvC_Eyr95bGir3p2_/UAq-fFEb66NWyvpV_pjM1_indicators-tab-alerts-subtab-add-time.png" alt caption size="37"}The time specified is in your local \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.d-airy}}'s time \{\{zone}}. 12:00 AM is the default selection, but you can select a time of day that best meets your \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.d-airy}}'s needs. In this example, 05:30 AM is selected.:inlineImage[]{src="https://archbee-image-uploads-qa.s3.amazonaws.com/QjalyvC_Eyr95bGir3p2_/V15TtOBKLY83J1LftltLE_indicators-tab-alerts-subtab-add-time-slct.png" alt caption size="42"}:::hint\{type="info"}
   **NOTE:&#x20;**&#x54;he time specified here combines with the period selected in the **Period Preferences** area in [Step 1: Enable & Set Up Health Indicators](Step1Indicators.htm), and these settings determine how often \{\{masterVAS-Products.Pulse-only}} examines \{\{PlatformContentSnippets.PerformanceIndicators-ThresholdDifferential}}s and notifies your \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.d-airy}} team. At the time specified, \{\{masterVAS-Products.Pulse-only}} examines the \{\{PlatformContentSnippets.PerformanceIndicators-ThresholdDifferential}}s for your \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.d-airy}} first and then alerts your \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.d-airy}} team after processing completes. Depending on the size of your \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.d-airy}} and the number of \{\{PlatformContentSnippets.PerformanceIndicators-EventIndicator}}s being tracked by this alert group, alerts may not be sent or received exactly at the time specified.
   \:::That's all there is to adding a new alert group in \{\{masterVAS-Products.Pulse-only}}, but don't forget to save your changes!
7. Click the **Save** button. \{\{masterVAS-Products.Pulse-only}} returns you to the \{\{Buttons-UI.AlertGroups}} page and confirms that your changes have been saved.:inlineImage[]{src="https://archbee-image-uploads-qa.s3.amazonaws.com/QjalyvC_Eyr95bGir3p2_/0vz1AVd-m_MYuOKazn1K9_indicators-tab-alerts-subtab-added.png" alt caption size="82"}

Now that you've successfully created an alert group that will notify your \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.d-airy}} team when an event occurs across your \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.h-erd}}, let's take a look at how these changes are displayed on the [Process Scheduler](../../../process-scheduler.mdx) page in \{\{masterVAS-Products.Pulse-only}}. Proceed to [Step 4: View Scheduled Alerts in Process Scheduler](step-4-view-scheduled-alerts-in-process-scheduler.mdx) to continue.
