<ExpandableHeading>
  ### GENEX DairySuite can now support third-party herd data!

  Do your customers use non-\{\{masterVAS.CompanyName-short}} herd management tools? They can still take full advantage of our powerful \{\{UDS-general.GENEX-Pulse}} to improve their herd's genetic process! Simply import their herd data into \{\{UDS-general.GENEX-Pulse}} and create customized genetic plans tailored to the dairy's goals. \{\{UDS-general.GENEX-Pulse}} will help you select the best sires and mating partners to improve specific traits, refine the herd, and drive long-term genetic progress.

  Visit [Add Third-Party Herd Data](../GettingStarted/UseThirdPartyData.htm) to get started!
</ExpandableHeading>

<ExpandableHeading>
  ### Linear mating is now available for third-party herds

  \{\{UDS-general.GENEX-Pulse}} now supports linear mating for herds that rely on linear scores to guide their genetic planning decisions! If you include phenotype data (or physical traits) for animals from a third-party herd, you can now mate them using their linear scores.

  When you build your genetic plan and get to :Link[Set Mating Options in Step 4]{label="Set Mating Options in Step 4" overridedLabel="Set Mating Options in Step 4" spaceId docId="Q6qb0xcUw4LyE67OtGMPX" version="v2" docAnchorId="#SetMatingOptions" loadingMethod="dynamic" newTab="false" githubPath="../BuildGeneticPlan/GeneticPlanStep4.htm#SetMatingOptions" href="../BuildGeneticPlan/GeneticPlanStep4.htm#SetMatingOptions"}, select **Linear Mate** to use phenotype data or **Linear/Pedigree** to use a combination of both phenotype and genomic data to match animals.
</ExpandableHeading>

<ExpandableHeading>
  ### GENEX now detects pedigree inconsistencies

  When you create or duplicate a plan, \{\{UDS-general.UDSBrand}} can now detect and alert you if pedigree information in \{\{masterVAS-Products.Pulse-only}} does not match the pedigree information in proofs. If you receive the warning, \{\{UDS-general.UDSBrand}} will use the pedigree information from the proofs. You can use the :Link[Data Integrity Report]{label="Data Integrity Report" overridedLabel="Data Integrity Report" spaceId docId="2vwexQ_em-anYfngtfeEw" version="v2" docAnchorId="#Data" loadingMethod="dynamic" newTab="false" githubPath="../Dashboard/Dashboard-GenPlan.htm#Data" href="genex-dairysuite-genetic-dashboard.mdx"} to check the discrepancy and correct the incorrect data in \{\{masterVAS-Products.Pulse-only}}.
</ExpandableHeading>

<ExpandableHeading>
  ### Expanded the genes and haplotypes list

  Additional haplotypes are now shown in \{\{masterVAS-Products.Pulse-only}} and used in the mating process, including JNS, the Jersey haplotype that affects neuropathy with splayed forelimbs, and BH14, the Brown Swiss haplotype that can cause early pregnancy loss. Adding this information helps the genetic planning tool eliminate poor matches that could carry forward recessive genes and haplotypes.
</ExpandableHeading>

  ### New Mating Options: Mate Red Females to Red Sires and Somatic Cell Score

  You now have even more mating options to target your herd's genetic goals! Enable **Mate Red Females to Red Sires Only** to prioritize red progeny; this option overrides other mating options to ensure that your herd's red genes are passed down.

  You can also set the **Somatic Cell Score** to prevent mating between a \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.s-ire}} and a female that could result in progeny with high somatic cell scores. Lower somatic cell scores can indicate that progeny will have less \{\{mastitis}} incidents, as well as maximized milk production and healthier milk quality.
