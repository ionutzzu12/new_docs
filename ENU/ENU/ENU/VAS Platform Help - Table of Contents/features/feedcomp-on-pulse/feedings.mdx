  ### FeedComp Rules for Handling Splits

  - Split feedings with no equipment change (described in Scenario :Link[1]{label="1" overridedLabel="1" spaceId docId="ZnD-2aIRbRK5oTUU59QGY" version="v2" docAnchorId="#splitfeedings-scenario1" loadingMethod="dynamic" newTab="false" href="feedings.mdx"}) behave the same was as standard scheduled feedings.
  - If a feeding is in progress or a split is completed, the only change allowed for the feeding is swapping equipment. Other updates can be made after the feeding schedule resets.
  - If the first split has not started when the equipment assigned to a split feeding is changed, the splits are recalculated.
  - Once the first split begins, \{\{masterVAS-Products.FeedComp}} does not recalculate the splits. Instead, it applies the outcomes described in Scenarios :Link[2]{label="2" overridedLabel="2" spaceId docId="ZnD-2aIRbRK5oTUU59QGY" version="v2" docAnchorId="#splitfeedings-scenario2" loadingMethod="dynamic" newTab="false" href="feedings.mdx"}, :Link[3]{label="3" overridedLabel="3" spaceId docId="ZnD-2aIRbRK5oTUU59QGY" version="v2" docAnchorId="#splitfeedings-scenario3" loadingMethod="dynamic" newTab="false" href="feedings.mdx"}, and :Link[4]{label="4" overridedLabel="4" spaceId docId="ZnD-2aIRbRK5oTUU59QGY" version="v2" docAnchorId="#splitfeedings-scenario4" loadingMethod="dynamic" newTab="false" href="feedings.mdx"} below.

  <ExpandableHeading>
    ### Scenario 1: Standard Split Feeding

    **When This Happens:&#x20;**&#x41; recipe load is bigger than the mixer capacity, and you approve it to be split before feeding starts.

    **What&#x20;**\{\{masterVAS-Products.FeedComp}}**&#x20;Does:**

    - Divides the load into equal parts
    - Recalculates ingredient amounts for each split
    - Updates \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.p-en}} counts

    **What You See in Feedings:**

    - Splits created as planned
    - Main feeding created and scheduled; splits viewable in :Link[split feeding details]{label="split feeding details" overridedLabel="split feeding details" spaceId docId="ZnD-2aIRbRK5oTUU59QGY" version="v2" docAnchorId="#splitfeedingdetails-viewsteps" loadingMethod="dynamic" newTab="false" href="feedings.mdx"}

    **What You See in&#x20;**:Link[Feeding Schedule Exports]{label="Feeding Schedule Exports" overridedLabel="Feeding Schedule Exports" spaceId docId="ZnD-2aIRbRK5oTUU59QGY" version="v2" docAnchorId="#export" loadingMethod="dynamic" newTab="false" href="feedings.mdx"}**:&#x20;**&#x41;ll splits listed individually and ready for feeding.

    **What You See in&#x20;**[Feed History](<../../../../../VAS Platform Help - Table of Contents/Features/FeedComp on PULSE/Reports/feed-history-report.mdx>)**:&#x20;**&#x53;plits reflect the actual feeding status (completed, incomplete, aborted, or missing).

    **Why It Matters:&#x20;**&#x59;ou get accurate splits without doing any math by hand. \{\{masterVAS-Products.FeedComp}} automatically splits the recipe load to safely fit within mixer capacity.
  </ExpandableHeading>

  <ExpandableHeading>
    ### Scenario 2: Splits Partially Completed, Equipment Changed, New Mixer Larger Than Remaining Load

    **When This Happens:&#x20;**&#x59;ou start with splits, then switch to a mixer larger than what’s left in the load.

    **What&#x20;**\{\{masterVAS-Products.FeedComp}}**&#x20;Does:**

    - Keeps completed and/or in-progress splits as is
    - Merges the remaining load into one final split

    **What You See in Feedings:**

    - Completed and/or in-progress splits remain with no changes
    - Remaining splits merged into one new split

    **What You See in&#x20;**[Feed History](<../../../../../VAS Platform Help - Table of Contents/Features/FeedComp on PULSE/Reports/feed-history-report.mdx>)**:&#x20;**&#x53;plits reflect the actual feeding status (completed, incomplete, aborted, or missing).

    **Why It Matters:&#x20;**\{\{masterVAS-Products.FeedComp}} reorganizes the feeding automatically; no manual adjustment needed.
  </ExpandableHeading>

  <ExpandableHeading>
    ### Scenario 3: Splits Partially Completed, Equipment Changed, New Mixer Smaller Than Remaining Load but Large Enough for Each Split

    **When This Happens:&#x20;**&#x59;ou switch to a smaller mixer that can’t hold the whole remaining load, but can hold each split.

    **What&#x20;**\{\{masterVAS-Products.FeedComp}}**&#x20;Does:**

    - Completed and/or in-progress splits are **Completed**
    - Leaves remaining as separate, and each recalculated as **Completed**

    **What You See in Feedings:**&#x41;ll splits remain as is.

    **What You See in&#x20;**[Feed History](<../../../../../VAS Platform Help - Table of Contents/Features/FeedComp on PULSE/Reports/feed-history-report.mdx>)**:&#x20;**&#x53;plits reflect the actual feeding status (completed, incomplete, aborted, or missing).

    **Why It Matters:&#x20;**&#x53;plits remain accurate without any recalculation required. In this case, you may choose to manually recalculate the remaining splits, depending on how much total load remains and how many \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.p-ens}} still need to be fed.
  </ExpandableHeading>

  <ExpandableHeading>
    ### Scenario 4: Splits Partially Completed, Equipment Changed, New Mixer Too Small for Remaining Load or Even a Single Split

    **When This Happens:&#x20;**&#x59;ou switch to a mixer that can’t hold the remaining load or even one split.

    **What&#x20;**\{\{masterVAS-Products.FeedComp}}**&#x20;Does:**

    - Completed and/or in-progress splits are **Completed**
    - Acknowledges that the chosen equipment cannot safely hold the remaining splits
    - Shows \{\{FeedComp.FeedCompOverCapacity}}\{\{Controls.ControlTypes}} for all remaining splits

    **What You See in Feedings:**

    - All splits remain as is
    - \{\{FeedComp.FeedCompOverCapacity}}\{\{Controls.ControlTypes}} displayed for the main feeding

    **What You See in&#x20;**:Link[Feeding Schedule Exports]{label="Feeding Schedule Exports" overridedLabel="Feeding Schedule Exports" spaceId docId="ZnD-2aIRbRK5oTUU59QGY" version="v2" docAnchorId="#export" loadingMethod="dynamic" newTab="false" href="feedings.mdx"}**:&#x20;**&#x41;n \{\{FeedComp.FeedCompOverCapacity}} warning displayed for all remaining splits.

    **What You See in&#x20;**[Feed History](<../../../../../VAS Platform Help - Table of Contents/Features/FeedComp on PULSE/Reports/feed-history-report.mdx>)**:&#x20;**&#x53;plits reflect the actual feeding status (completed, incomplete, aborted, or missing).

    **Why It Matters:&#x20;**\{\{masterVAS-Products.FeedComp}} prevents errors by flagging when the mixer is too small for the remaining splits. Similar to Scenario :Link[3]{label="3" overridedLabel="3" spaceId docId="ZnD-2aIRbRK5oTUU59QGY" version="v2" docAnchorId="#splitfeedings-scenario3" loadingMethod="dynamic" newTab="false" href="feedings.mdx"}, in this case you may choose to manually recalculate the remaining splits, depending on how much total load remains and how many \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.p-ens}} still need to be fed.
  </ExpandableHeading>
