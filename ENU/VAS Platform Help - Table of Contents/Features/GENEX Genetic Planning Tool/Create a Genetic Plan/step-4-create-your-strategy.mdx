Create Your Strategy is the fourth and final step of the planning process. In this step, you can set the current prices of semen, bull and beef calves, as well as the latest conception and cull rates for the \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.h-erd}}. You can also control which females in your \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.h-erd}} receive each type of semen (Sexed, Conventional, and Beef).

At the top of the page, you can see a brief summary of your plan's projected costs and requirements, as well as determine whether you want to maintain the current size of your \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.h-erd}} or if you want to expand your current number of milking females. If you're unsure about your current settings, you can even create alternative scenarios to ensure you're making the right choices for your \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.h-erd}}. \{\{masterVAS-Products.Pulse-only}} then incorporates your input and generates a report with the proposed costs and \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.h-erd}} implications of the plan.

        ### Learn more about each semen type

        These are three standard types of semen available:

        <ExpandableHeading>
          ### Sexed semen

          Sexed semen dramatically increases the chance of having a female calf (from 49% to 90+%). If you are trying to expand the \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.h-erd}} and need a large number of replacement female calves, increasing this value will make achieving your goal easier. Also, because the bulls typically used for sexed semen tend to be high-quality, the female calves resulting from the mating will continue to improve the genetics of the \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.h-erd}}.
        </ExpandableHeading>

        <ExpandableHeading>
          ### Conventional semen

          The largest portion of the \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.h-erd}} is bred using conventional semen \{\{due}} to cost savings of using conventional semen instead of sexed semen. Using conventional semen typically results in pregnancies with 49% female calves. Due to the additional cost of sexed semen, most \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.d-airies}} tend to use conventional semen for most of their breeding activity.
        </ExpandableHeading>

        <ExpandableHeading>
          ### Beef semen

          When animals are bred with beef semen, the resulting calves will be sold and not be added to the \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.h-erd}}. Typically, females bred with beef semen are below average genetically and tend to be older animals.
        </ExpandableHeading>

  ### How are conception and cull rates determined?

  <Paragraph indent="1">
    **Conception Rate:**\{\{masterVAS-Products.Pulse-only}} automatically populates conception rates if they exist in the system and are greater than 0. If no rates are available in \{\{masterVAS-Products.Pulse-only}}, the system uses values that were entered in a previous genetic plan. If there are no previous plans available, \{\{masterVAS-Products.Pulse-only}} inserts industry-standard conception rates.
    **Cull Rate:** The cull rate is the percentage of animals that were SOLD or DIED per year. If available, \{\{UDS-general.UDSBrand}} analyzes the last three years' worth of \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.d-airy}} event data for heifers and the last year's worth of data for cows.
  </Paragraph>

  <Paragraph listStyleType="disc" indent="2">
    If the \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.d-airy}} has fewer than three years' worth of event data for heifers, \{\{masterVAS-Products.Pulse-only}} includes events from the last year or all available events, whichever is greater.
  </Paragraph>

  <Paragraph listStyleType="disc" indent="2">
    If the \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.d-airy}} does not capture SOLD or DIED events, \{\{masterVAS-Products.Pulse-only}} inserts industry-standard cull rates.
    \:::
  </Paragraph>

  ### Help me choose

  <Paragraph indent="1">
    Several Mating Types are available:
  </Paragraph>

  <Paragraph listStyleType="disc" indent="2">
    **Genomic & Pedigree:** Mating assignments will be based on the plan goals and traits identified as important for this genetic plan.
  </Paragraph>

  <Paragraph listStyleType="disc" indent="2">
    **Reverse Mate:** This mating process is also known by the \{\{UDS-general.UDSBrand}} brand name, Bolt, and is intended to find bulls that should NOT be used for mating with specific females. That includes bulls that score high for haplotypes, recessive traits, and inbreeding. The least desirable bulls are returned as \{\{SIR1}}, \{\{SIR2}}, and \{\{SIR3}} for Reverse Mate genetic plans. Some \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.d-airies}} choose to use this method to simplify the breeding process and make it more efficient, while also minimizing the risks of inbreeding. The \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.d-airy}} worker handling breeding tasks only has to worry about which bulls NOT to use with specific females.
  </Paragraph>

  <Paragraph listStyleType="disc" indent="2">
    **Linear Mate:** This mating process is only available for third-party herds who have shared phenotype data (physical traits). Mating assignments are based on animals' linear scores, which are calculated using phenotype data. If phenotype data is missing or incomplete when you select this mating type, \{\{UDS-general.GENEX-Pulse}} can use genomic data to fill in any gaps.
  </Paragraph>

  <Paragraph listStyleType="disc" indent="2">
    **Linear/Pedigree:** This mating process is only available for third-party herds who have shared phenotype data (physical traits). Mating assignments are based on both an animals' linear scores (phenotype data) and pedigree information (genomic data).
    \:::
  </Paragraph>

  ### Help me choose

  <Paragraph indent="1">
    All animals except \{\{DNB}} and pregnant animals are included by default. Click the toggle to disable or enable animals by reproductive code (\{\{RC}}):
  </Paragraph>

  <Paragraph listStyleType="disc" indent="2">
    Include Heifers No Status (RC0): If enabled, include heifers in the genetic plan. In \{\{DC-UI.DC-ProductName}}, heifers have a reproductive code of 0 (\{\{RC=0}}).
  </Paragraph>

  <Paragraph listStyleType="disc" indent="2">
    Include Do Not Breed (DNB) Animals (RC1): If enabled, include DNB animals in the genetic plan. In \{\{DC-UI.DC-ProductName}}, DNB animals have a reproductive code of 0 (\{\{RC=1}}).
  </Paragraph>

  <Paragraph listStyleType="disc" indent="2">
    Include \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.F-resh-cow-}} Cows (RC2): If enabled, include \{\{Events.1FRESH}} cows in the genetic plan. In \{\{DC-UI.DC-ProductName}}, \{\{Events.1FRESH}} cows have a reproductive code of 2 (\{\{RC=2}}). \{\{Events.1FRESH}} cows have recently given birth to a calf. Depending on the time frame of the genetic plan, currently \{\{Events.1FRESH}} cows may become eligible to be bred for their next calf.
  </Paragraph>

  <Paragraph listStyleType="disc" indent="2">

  <ExpandableHeading>
    ### How are conception and cull rates determined?

    **Conception Rate:**\{\{masterVAS-Products.Pulse-only}} automatically populates conception rates if they exist in the system and are greater than 0. If no rates are available in \{\{masterVAS-Products.Pulse-only}}, the system uses values that were entered in a previous genetic plan. If there are no previous plans available, \{\{masterVAS-Products.Pulse-only}} inserts industry-standard conception rates.

    **Cull Rate:** The cull rate is the percentage of animals that were SOLD or DIED per year. If available, \{\{UDS-general.UDSBrand}} analyzes the last three years' worth of \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.d-airy}} event data for heifers and the last year's worth of data for cows.

    - If the \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.d-airy}} has fewer than three years' worth of event data for heifers, \{\{masterVAS-Products.Pulse-only}} includes events from the last year or all available events, whichever is greater.
    - If the \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.d-airy}} does not capture SOLD or DIED events, \{\{masterVAS-Products.Pulse-only}} inserts industry-standard cull rates.
  </ExpandableHeading>

  <ExpandableHeading>
    ### Learn more about Optional Values

    - **Abort Heifers:** Percentage of heifer pregnancies that end due to an abort.
    - **Abort Cows:** Percentage of cow pregnancies that end due to an abort.
    - **DOA (Females) Heifers:** Percentage of dead on arrival female calves delivered by heifers.
    - **DOA (Females) Cows:** Percentage of dead on arrival female calves delivered by cows.
    - **Pregnant Heifers Cull Rate:** Rate at which pregnant heifers are culled from the \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.h-erd}}.
    - **Pregnant Cows Cull Rate:** Rate at which pregnant cows are culled from the \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.h-erd}}.

    <ExpandableHeading>
      ### How are optional values calculated and pre-populated?

      - **Abort Heifers and Cows:**\{\{UDS-general.UDSBrand}}\{\{UDS-general.UDSProduct}} evaluates \{\{masterVAS-Products.Pulse-only}} abort events for heifers and cows in the last 365 days and calculates the rate as follows: Abort % = (total aborts) / (total pregnant animals)
      - **DOA and Pregnant Cull Rates:&#xA0;**\{\{masterVAS-Products.Pulse-only}} automatically populates the DOA and Pregnant Cull rates from the last genetic plan if the values are greater than 0. If there are no previous plans available, \{\{masterVAS-Products.Pulse-only}} inserts industry-standard values.
    </ExpandableHeading>

  ## Bulls Restrictions

  Use this option to allow whether each bull in a mating plan can appear as only the first, second, or third choice (SIR1, SIR2, or SIR3) or a combination for female groups. You can use this option to ensure that you have an even distribution for all bulls or you can match the best bull to the best female.

  Follow the steps below to continue:

  <Image src="https://archbee-image-uploads-qa.s3.amazonaws.com/QjalyvC_Eyr95bGir3p2_/WCex1KcIkH2WXxvZedyT6_step4-restrict-bulls.png" size="60" isUploading="false" initialPath="../../../Resources/Images/PlatformAddons/GenPlanning/GenPlan/step4-restrict-bulls.png" githubPath="Content/Resources/Images/PlatformAddons/GenPlanning/GenPlan/step4-restrict-bulls.png" position="flex-start" showCaption="false"></Image>

  1. Click Options button

  <Image src="https://archbee-image-uploads-qa.s3.amazonaws.com/QjalyvC_Eyr95bGir3p2_/wIXwfkZ07oPcd_rKpJE8J_options-green-small41x29.png" size="10" caption="Options button" alt="Options button" isUploading="false" initialPath="../../../Resources/Images/icons/options-green-small_41x29.png" githubPath="Content/Resources/Images/icons/options-green-small_41x29.png" position="flex-start" showCaption="true" indent="2"></Image>

  <Paragraph indent="1">
    and select **Bulls Restrictions**.
  </Paragraph>

  2. Click **Add Restriction**.
  3. Select the **Female Group** that will be limited to these bull selections.
  4. Select the bulls you want to limit to first, second, or third choice or a combination.
  5. Check the boxes to denote whether the selected bulls should be selected as a first choice, second choice, and/or third choice.
  6. Set the minimum and maximum unit or percentage limits for the likeliness that the selected bull(s) will be chosen as the first, second, or third choice. The minimum value must be less than or equal to the maximum value. If you use percentage values, neither value can exceed or add up to 100%.
  7. Save your changes.

  Follow the guidelines below when setting restrictions:

  - If you only check the **First Choice Available** box for a bull, the bull will never be chosen as a second or third choice. Likewise, if you only check **Second Choice Available** box for a bull, the bull will never be chosen as a first or third choice.
  - Restrictions are not available for reverse matings.
  - If you add a restriction to a dairy bull, you should not add the same restriction to a beef bull. Conversely, if you add a restriction to a beef bull, you should not add the same restriction to a dairy bull.
  - For example, if you want to select a beef bull as the first choice for sexed females, you should not add a dairy bull as the first, second, or third choice for sexed females.
