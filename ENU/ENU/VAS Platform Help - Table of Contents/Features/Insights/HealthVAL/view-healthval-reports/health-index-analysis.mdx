Use the \{\{Insights.HealthIndexAnalysis}} page to review animals that exceed your \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.h-erd}}'s \{\{Health Index Threshold}}, which is set on the :Link[Customize > Benchmark Settings]{label="Customize > Benchmark Settings" overridedLabel="Customize > Benchmark Settings" spaceId docId="4x4uiAjSMUlmI9nAt47wP" version="v2" docAnchorId="#Benchmark" loadingMethod="dynamic" newTab="false" githubPath="CustomizeHealthVAL.htm#Benchmark" href="../../healthval/customize-healthval.mdx"} page.

Animals with a higher Health Index have been diagnosed with more health events than others of similar \{\{age}}, and this summary allows you to quickly identify animals that may need to be culled. For instance, if an early-lactation cow has already had multiple cases of \{\{mastitis}}, her index value will be high, and you may determine that she is susceptible to illness and decide to sell her.

See the guidelines below when reviewing your \{\{Insights.HealthIndexAnalysis}} summary:

1. Select **Filters** to open a side panel on the left of the page to filter the list of animals using a threshold value. Once a filter is selected, the current filter applied is displayed next to the **Filters** option. Click the **X** on a specific filter to remove it.
2. Click the **Customize** button to edit your \{\{Insights.HealthSummaries}} summaries for cow and heifer events and/or modify your \{\{Insights.HealthSummaries}} benchmark settings.
3. **Reference** checkboxes allow you to control which lines are plotted on the graph. Each reference is color-coded to match a specific bar or line on the graph. Check a box to include a plot line on the graph. Uncheck a box to remove a plot line from the graph.
4. Hover over an animal's data point on the graph to view additional details. Or, click the animal's data point on the graph to open a side panel and view even more details about the animal, including recent events. See [View Animal Details](../../../DairyComp/MyDC/animals/view-animal-details.mdx) to learn more about the information displayed in the side panel.
5. Scroll down to view the graphed data in a table format.
