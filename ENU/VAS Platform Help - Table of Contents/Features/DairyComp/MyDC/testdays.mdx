  ## SCC

  Check the **SCC** tab to track the somatic cell count in your \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.h-erd}}'s milk from a single \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.t-estday}}. SCC is an indicator of milk quality, as most somatic cells found in milk are white blood cells, which are produced as an immune response to an infection. Generally, lower SCC values point to better animal health and milk quality.

  - \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.H-erd-}}**&#x20;Somatic Cell Count Distribution:&#x20;**&#x55;se this bar graph to view the SCC in your \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.h-erd}}'s milk. Hover over the different colors of each bar to view the amount of animals that have the corresponding SCC. You can also hover over any point on the % of \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.H-erd-}} line to view the percentage of animals in the \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.h-erd}} that produce milk with a high SCC.:::hint\{type="info"}
    **NOTE:&#x20;**&#x41;n SCC value over 200,000 is considered high. This value is shown by the system as "200" because it's divided by 1000 to make it easier to manage.
    \:::
  - **Estimated SCC% Contribution by&#x20;**\{\{masterVAS-Terminology.P-en-}}**:&#x20;**&#x54;he table displays SCC information for the entire \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.h-erd}} by \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.p-en}}. On this table, you can view the average SCC on a per-animal basis, the percent of the \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.p-en}} with a SCC>200, and the actual number of animals that have a high SCC in the \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.p-en}}. Additionally, you can see the exact percentage that each \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.p-en}} contributes to the \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.h-erd}}'s total SCC to quickly determine whether a single \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.p-en}} is responsible for an elevated SCC. You can also see the linear score for the \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.p-en}}. (The linear score is a log of somatic cell count that increases the accuracy of calculations containing somatic cell count).
  - **Estimated SCC% Contribution by Animal:&#x20;**&#x54;he table displays SCC data for each animal in the \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.h-erd}}. On this table, you can view straightforward information like the animal's current SCC and the number of tests where the animal had a high SCC (more than 200,000 somatic cells/mL milk). Additionally, you can see how the animal compares to the rest of the \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.h-erd}} by viewing its relative value and linear score. You can also see the exact percentage that each animal contributes to the \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.h-erd}}'s total SCC to quickly determine the individual animal’s impact on the overall milk quality.

  ## MUN

  Check the \{\{MUN}} tab to monitor the urea nitrogen level in your \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.h-erd}}'s milk on a single \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.t-estday}}. Milk urea nitrogen values are used to monitor the way an animal utilizes nutrients from the diet. Urea is produced in the liver from ammonia derived from the breakdown of protein in the rumen and from normal daily metabolism. If bacteria in the rumen cannot capture the ammonia and convert it to protein, the excess ammonia is absorbed and converted to urea to prevent toxicity. This urea is excreted in urine, manure, and milk, and can be quantified in the \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.t-estday}} milk samples.

  - \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.H-erd-}}**&#x20;Milk Urea Nitrogen Distribution:&#x20;**&#x55;se this bar graph to view the distribution of MUN values on a \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.h-erd}}-level basis. Hover over the colored bars to see the number of animals in each lactation group that produced the corresponding MUN value.
  - **Average MUN by&#x20;**\{\{masterVAS-Terminology.P-en-}}**:** The table displays the average MUN value on a \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.p-en}} basis, along with corresponding DIM and milk production data.
  - **Average Days in Milk by MUN:** The table displays the corresponding average DIM for the range of MUN values, categorized by lactation group. The MUN value is listed at the top of the table and the lactation groups are listed on the left side of the table. The corresponding average DIM is listed in the table.
</ExpandableHeading>

  ## Mature Equivalent

  Check the Mature Equivalent tab to view your \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.h-erd}}'s Mature Equivalent data. The Mature Equivalent is a standardization tool that projects fat, protein, and milk production values when the lactation record is adjusted for \{\{age}} at \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.f-reshening}}, frequency of milking, and stage of lactation. This allows for comparisons between lactation groups and helps project the future direction of the \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.h-erd}}’s performance.

  - \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.H-erd-}}**&#x20;Mature Equivalent Milk Trends:&#x20;**&#x54;he line graph displays the calculated mature equivalent values by lactation group over time. Each line represents a different lactation group. Hover over the points of the line to view the calculated Mature Equivalent value by the corresponding lactation group during each month.
  - **Mature Equivalent Rank Distribution:&#x20;**&#x55;se this graph to identify how many animals fall in each ranking category (A, B, C and D) for milk, fat, and protein. Hover over the different colored horizontal bars to view the exact number of animals for each rank:\* **Rank A:&#x20;**&#x52;anks at least 10% above the average herdmates.
    - **Rank B:&#x20;**&#x52;anks between 1 to 10% above the average herdmates.
    - **Rank C:&#x20;**&#x52;anks between 0 to -9% below the average herdmates.
    - **Rank D:&#x20;**&#x52;anks at least -10% below the average herdmates.
  - **Mature Equivalent Rankings:&#x20;**&#x55;se this table to view an animal's Mature Equivalent values including milk, fat, and protein, as well as her mature equivalent ranking against her herdmates.
</ExpandableHeading>

  - **Avg % Fat:&#x20;**&#x41;verage percentage of milkfat produced by the animal.

  - \{\{305ME}}**&#x20;Milk:&#x20;**&#x33;05 day mature \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.h-erd}} equivalent milk production.
  - **Relative Value:&#x20;**&#x41; percentage of the animal's 305ME Milk value normalized to the \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.H-erd-}} Average 305ME Milk for that \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.t-estday}}.
