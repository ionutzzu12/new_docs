Use the **Inventory** dashboard to quickly view your current inventory across all \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.d-airies}} in clear, easy-to-follow graphs. Use the data to identify, review, and analyze current inventory trends. You can switch between tabs to see the breakdown of your inventory in different ways: by lactation stage, breed, reproduction status, changes in the \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.h-erd}}, health events, or \{\{age}} and lactation group.

<hint type="info">
  **NOTE:&#x20;**&#x55;pdate your inventory data on a daily basis to ensure that your inventory graphs are always accurate and up-to-date.
</hint>

Follow the guidelines below when working with Inventory graphs:

- **Filters:** Click the **Filters** option on the upper left side of the dashboard to open a side panel where you can manage the data you see in the graph:\* **Facility filters:&#x20;**&#x55;se the **Facility Type** and/or **Facilities** filters to manage the facilities shown in the graph. Facilities are listed by \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.d-airy}} name. By default, the graph includes data from all \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.d-airies}}.
  - **Date Range filter:&#x20;**&#x55;se the **Date&#x20;****From** and **Date&#x20;****To** filters to only view data from that range. By default, the graph includes data from the previous seven days.
- **Tooltips:** Hover over the graph data to view more detailed information, including animal counts and percentages.
- **Views:** On some inventory dashboards, you can change how the graph displays data. Select different views on the upper right side of the dashboard, where available.

<ExpandableHeading>
  ## Lactation Stage

  Use this tab to view animals sorted by their lactation status.

  <Image src="https://archbee-image-uploads-qa.s3.amazonaws.com/QjalyvC_Eyr95bGir3p2_/q6f8pIkC7t3nKzXgPzf65_inventory-lactation.png" size="79" isUploading="false" initialPath="../../../../Resources/Images/Platform/oneview/inventory-lactation.png" githubPath="Content/Resources/Images/Platform/oneview/inventory-lactation.png" position="flex-start" showCaption="false"></Image>

  The data is separated into two graphs: one for heifers (Lactation 0) and one for cows (Lactation > 0). The graphs show average values, which are calculated from the data collected every day during the selected date range.
</ExpandableHeading>

<ExpandableHeading>
  ## Breed

  Use this tab to view animals sorted by breed.

  <Image src="https://archbee-image-uploads-qa.s3.amazonaws.com/QjalyvC_Eyr95bGir3p2_/Hz0_OQhISHi3WU5edYLz4_inventory-breed.png" size="97" isUploading="false" initialPath="../../../../Resources/Images/Platform/oneview/inventory-breed.png" githubPath="Content/Resources/Images/Platform/oneview/inventory-breed.png" position="flex-start" showCaption="false"></Image>

  Change the **View** to see the average number of animals in each breed by Animal Group (number of lactations) or by Breed during the selected date range. The graphs show average values, which are calculated from the data collected every day during the selected date range:

  - In Animal Group view, each bar in the graph represents the average number of animals in each breed sorted by lactation group.
  - In Breed view, you can only view data for one breed at a time. Select the breed from the drop-down list in the upper left corner. By default, the pie chart displays data for Holsteins or the company's most popular breed.
    The animal count of the selected breed is shown in two separate pie charts: Animal Group, where each slice represents a lactation group, and \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.D-airies-}}, where each slice represents a \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.d-airy}}.

  <Image src="https://archbee-image-uploads-qa.s3.amazonaws.com/QjalyvC_Eyr95bGir3p2_/QkKJ3nVDAeXNYpCLW3ui7_inventory-breed-view.png" size="80" isUploading="false" initialPath="../../../../Resources/Images/Platform/oneview/inventory-breed-view.png" githubPath="Content/Resources/Images/Platform/oneview/inventory-breed-view.png" position="flex-start" showCaption="false" indent="2"></Image>
</ExpandableHeading>

<ExpandableHeading>
  ## Reproduction Status

  Use this tab to view animals sorted by their reproduction status:

  <Image src="https://archbee-image-uploads-qa.s3.amazonaws.com/QjalyvC_Eyr95bGir3p2_/HRcrmXd7Gvssu10TLi0X9_inventory-reprostatus.png" size="88" isUploading="false" initialPath="../../../../Resources/Images/Platform/oneview/inventory-reprostatus.png" githubPath="Content/Resources/Images/Platform/oneview/inventory-reprostatus.png" position="flex-start" showCaption="false"></Image>

  Change the **View** to see the average number of animals in each reproduction status by Lactations or by \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.D-airies-}} during the selected date range. The graphs show average values, which are calculated from the data collected every day during the selected date range:

  - In Lactations view, each bar in the graph represents a lactation group's animal count sorted by reproduction status.
  - In \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.D-airies-}} view, each bar in the graph represents a \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.d-airy}}'s animal count sorted by reproduction status.
</ExpandableHeading>

<ExpandableHeading>
  ## Changes in the Herd

  Use this tab to view animals sorted by changes in the \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.h-erd}}. The graphs illustrate the number of animals that underwent any of the following changes: Born, Died, Enrollment, Sold, Go Home, Departure, Dry, or \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.F-resh-cow-}}.

  <Image src="https://archbee-image-uploads-qa.s3.amazonaws.com/QjalyvC_Eyr95bGir3p2_/sCk1nmYuuPDpQNEHwXh7X_inventory-changes.png" size="83" isUploading="false" initialPath="../../../../Resources/Images/Platform/oneview/inventory-changes.png" githubPath="Content/Resources/Images/Platform/oneview/inventory-changes.png" position="flex-start" showCaption="false"></Image>

  <hint type="info">
    **NOTE:&#x20;**&#x44;eparture includes all animals that were Sold, Died, or Go Home animals. Go Home includes all calves that have returned from the Heifer Grower facility to the \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.d-airy}}. Enrollment includes all animals that have an \{\{EDAT}} (enrollment date) within the selected date range.
  </hint>

  Change the **View** to see changes in the \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.h-erd}} by Animal Group (lactation number) or by \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.D-airies-}}:

  - In Animal Group view, each bar in the graph represents the number of animals in each lactation group that underwent the listed change.
  - In \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.D-airies-}} view, each bar in the graph represents the number of animals in a \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.d-airy}} that underwent the listed change.
</ExpandableHeading>

<ExpandableHeading>
  ## Health Events

  Use this tab to view health event data for your animals. After identifying health event trends in your inventory, you can use [HealthVAL](<../../../../../VAS Platform Help - Table of Contents/Features/Insights/healthval.mdx>) to learn more about the diseases affecting your \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.h-erd}} and take necessary measures to prevent or treat illnesses at your \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.d-airy}}.

  <Image src="https://archbee-image-uploads-qa.s3.amazonaws.com/QjalyvC_Eyr95bGir3p2_/YwlvJYmfu3MgEFT1ETGsv_inventory-health.png" size="80" isUploading="false" initialPath="../../../../Resources/Images/Platform/oneview/inventory-health.png" githubPath="Content/Resources/Images/Platform/oneview/inventory-health.png" position="flex-start" showCaption="false"></Image>

  Change the **View** to see health event data for All Animals or \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.F-resh-cow-}} Animals. The system will show different health events for All or \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.F-resh-cow-}} animals, as some conditions are only diagnosed in \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.f-resh-cow}} animals. For example, \{\{retained placenta}} only affects \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.f-resh-cow}} animals, so that event is only shown in the \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.F-resh-cow-}} Animals view.
</ExpandableHeading>

<ExpandableHeading>
  ## Age and Lactation Group

  Use this tab to view animals sorted by age or by lactation group.

  <Image src="https://archbee-image-uploads-qa.s3.amazonaws.com/QjalyvC_Eyr95bGir3p2_/7fek9_dCQCLaMf-aaiE46_inventory-agelact.png" size="90" isUploading="false" initialPath="../../../../Resources/Images/Platform/oneview/inventory-agelact.png" githubPath="Content/Resources/Images/Platform/oneview/inventory-agelact.png" position="flex-start" showCaption="false"></Image>

  Change the **View** to see the average number of animals in each age and lactation group for Cows, Heifers, or Males during the selected date range. The graphs show average values, which are calculated from the data collected every day during the selected date range:

  - In Cows view, the number of animals in each \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.d-airy}} are sorted by lactation group.
  - In Heifers or Males view, the number of animals in each \{\{masterVAS-Terminology.d-airy}} are sorted by age in days.
</ExpandableHeading>
