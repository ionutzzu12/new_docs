  ## Search and Filter the Load Error Report

  Options to search and filter the \{\{FeedComp.FeedCompReportNames}}\{\{Controls.ControlTypes}} are available at the top of the \{\{General.UI-gui}}. Follow the guidelines below when \{\{Commands.UserActions}} the \{\{FeedComp.FeedCompReportNames}}\{\{Controls.ControlTypes}}:

  - \{\{Commands.Click}}**Filters** at the top left of the page to limit the data that is displayed. The **Filters** side panel opens.
  - Use the **From** and **To** filters to only view data from that range.
  - \{\{Commands.UserActions}} a \{\{FeedComp.FeedCompFeatures-Upper}} to limit the \{\{Controls.ControlTypes}} to a specific \{\{FeedComp.FeedCompFeatures-Lower}}. Multiple \{\{FeedComp.FeedCompFeatures-Lower}} can be \{\{Commands.UserActions}}. You can also type text directly in the field to search for \{\{FeedComp.FeedCompFeatures-Lower}} by name.
  - \{\{Commands.UserActions}} an\{\{FeedComp.FeedCompFeatures-Upper}} to limit the \{\{Controls.ControlTypes}} to a specific \{\{FeedComp.FeedCompFeatures-Lower}}. Multiple \{\{FeedComp.FeedCompFeatures-Lower}} can be \{\{Commands.UserActions}}. You can also type text directly in the field to search for \{\{FeedComp.FeedCompFeatures-Lower}} by name.
  - \{\{Commands.UserActions}} a \{\{FeedComp.FeedCompFeatures-Upper}} to limit the \{\{Controls.ControlTypes}} to a specific \{\{FeedComp.FeedCompFeatures-Lower}}. Multiple \{\{FeedComp.FeedCompFeatures-Lower}} can be \{\{Commands.UserActions}}. You can also type text directly in the field to search for \{\{FeedComp.FeedCompFeatures-Lower}} by name.
  - Enter the preferred \{\{FeedComp.FeedCompFeatures-Upper}}**&#x20;Tolerance Error**. The error tolerance sets the minimum error amount to include in the report. For instance, if you set the error tolerance to 100, the report includes errors of 100 pounds and more (or kilograms based on the unit of measure specified for your [user account](../../Config/EditUserAcct.htm)).
  - \{\{Commands.UserActions}} the **Clear Filter\*\*\*\*s** option to clear all of the filters currently applied.

<ExpandableHeading indent="2">
  ## View Feeding Cards

  <hint type="info">
    **TIP:&#x20;**\{\{Controls.ControlTypes}} are displayed in pounds or kilograms based on the unit of measure specified for your [user account](../../Config/EditUserAcct.htm).
  </hint>

  Follow the guidelines below when \{\{Commands.UserActions}}\{\{FeedComp.FeedCompFeatures-Lower}}\{\{Controls.ControlTypes}}:

  - Date: The date of the \{\{FeedComp.FeedCompFeatures-Lower}} is displayed at the top left of the \{\{General.UI-gui}}.
  - \{\{FeedComp.FeedCompIngredient-singular}}: The name of the \{\{FeedComp.FeedCompFeatures-Lower}} used in the mix.
  - \{\{FeedComp.FeedCompLocation-singular}}: The name of the location where the ingredient was stored when the feeding was mixed.
  - \{\{FeedComp.FeedCompFeeder}}: The name of the \{\{FeedComp.FeedCompFeatures-Lower}} who loaded the ingredients into the \{\{mixer}}.
  - \{\{FeedComp.FeedCompFeeding-singular}} Name: The name of the \{\{FeedComp.FeedCompFeatures-Lower}}.
  - Target / AF Amount: The target and the total as-fed amounts for the \{\{FeedComp.FeedCompFeatures-Lower}}.
  - Error Amount: The total \{\{Commands.UserActions}} amount for the \{\{FeedComp.FeedCompFeatures-Lower}}.:::ExpandableHeading\{customAnchorSlug="erroramounts"}

  ### Click here to learn more about load error amounts!

  <Paragraph listStyleType="disc" indent="2">
    **Red:&#x20;**&#x41;mounts displayed in **red** indicate that the weight of the \{\{Commands.UserActions}} was at least 50 pounds (or 20 kilograms) higher than the load tolerance threshold allowed for the feeding.
  </Paragraph>

  <Paragraph listStyleType="disc" indent="2">
    **Orange:&#x20;**&#x41;mounts displayed in **orange** indicate that the weight of the \{\{Commands.UserActions}} was at least 50 pounds (or 20 kilograms) lower than the load tolerance threshold allowed for the feeding.
  </Paragraph>

  <Paragraph listStyleType="disc" indent="2">
    **Black:&#x20;**&#x41;mounts displayed in **black** indicate that the weight of the \{\{Commands.UserActions}} was within the load tolerance threshold allowed for the feeding.
    \:::
  </Paragraph>
